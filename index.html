Projects

1. Thumb Movement Analysis Using Cadaveric Specimens

Objective: To quantify thumb tip trajectories produced by thumb muscles, contributing to the understanding of musculoskeletal biomechanics and potential improvements in surgical outcomes.

Methods: Developed a motion tracking system and an arm fixation device. Conducted experiments using cadaveric specimens to measure muscle-induced movements. Analyzed the trajectory and path length of thumb tip movements caused by various muscles.

Results: Identified specific movement patterns for different thumb muscles. Highlighted the complex interaction between muscle function and thumb movement. Contributed data for refining models of thumb mechanics and potential applications in surgical procedures.

Documentation: View Project Poster

2. EEG Data Analysis Project

Objective: To analyze EEG data for extracting frequency bands and understanding brain activity patterns.

Methods: Developed MATLAB scripts for calculating band powers and analyzing frequency spectrums. Applied signal processing techniques to interpret EEG data.

Code Files: calculate_band_powers.m, print_trial_band_power.m, get_frequency_spectrum.m

3. Molecular Dynamic Simulation of Covalently Modified Protein Tyrosine Phosphatase

Objective: To analyze the physical movements of atoms and molecules through computer simulations, focusing on protein tyrosine phosphatase and its interaction with a ligand.

Methods: Used Gaussian software for ligand construction. Performed molecular dynamics simulations to study protein-ligand interactions.

Results: Found that the modified protein showed different stability and regional mobility compared to the wild type. Provided insights into the molecular stability of the protein-ligand complex.

Documentation: View Project Poster
